Questions, answered

The details.

Is my audio stored?

By default, each dictation is retained locally as a WAV under ~/.golos/recordings/ for benchmarking and troubleshooting. Turn off keep-recordings to discard it after processing. Text history stays in a local JSONL file you own.

What does it cost?

golos is free and open-source. Local speech-to-text costs nothing. Optional cloud transcription costs fractions of a cent per minute, and the built-in benchmark lets you compare supported models using your own voice.

Why does it need permissions?

Microphone lets golos hear you, Input Monitoring sees the global fn key, and Accessibility inserts text and reads permitted context. Without Accessibility it can still transcribe, but cannot paste for you.

Is Windows supported?

Not planned. golos is built around macOS-native Accessibility, keyboard-event, and camera-notch APIs. It is a Mac app through and through.

How does the dictionary learn?

When you edit inserted text, golos notices the near-match and suggests a correction. Promote it to your dictionary with a single confirmation or dismiss it. Nothing is learned without your approval.
